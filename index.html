Partial Demolition of Bushwick Mansion Sparks Conversation about Conservation

by Cai Pigliucci

Brooklyn, NY, Dec. 11, 2020

When boards went up and demolition began at the Lindemann House in November, some members of the community took notice, renewing Bushwick's discussion on landmarking.

The above map details the historic districts in Brooklyn and the landmarks in Bushwick grouped by the decade they were designated. Click to view designation details.

Landmark Preservation Commission was founded in 1965.

The mansion, built in 1890, is a part of a series of historic housing along Bushwick Ave. that have been studied since the early 1970s by the Landmarks Preservation Commission for landmarking and historic districting. Unlike neighboring districts in Brooklyn, Bushwick has no designated historic districts. Some say this is due to a lack in community interest.

View of the Lindemann House from across the street. Nov. 25, 2020 by Cai Pigliucci

“Like many areas in New York City that are dealing with bigger issues, like housing and income disparity, landmarking has never been their priority,” said Kelly Carroll, director of advocacy and community outreach at the Historic Districts Council.

Community members gather outside the mansion after the roof was partially destoryed in the first stage of demolition. Dec. 2, 2020 by Cai Pigliucci

"I feel like this mansion...it’s reawakened everyone," said Carroll. "The research is done, all that is required is the political will to make this happen."
